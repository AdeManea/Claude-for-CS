# Customer-Facing Summary Templates

**File:** `reference/customer-summary-templates.md`  
**Skill:** `csm:success-plan-progress-review`  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Purpose:** Customer-facing language templates by milestone status mix (positive, cautionary, escalation tones); QBR pre-work note structure and language guidance; language rules and placeholder conventions.

---

## 1. Language Guidelines

These guidelines apply to all customer-facing content generated by this skill. They govern tone, vocabulary, and framing regardless of which template is used.

### Always

- Write in the customer's perspective — focus on their outcomes, not the CSM's activities
- Use plain business language — avoid internal jargon (e.g., "OCV", "MoS", "safe_account", "progress review artifact")
- Name the value the customer has experienced or is working toward — this is what they care about
- Be specific where data is available — "your team has reduced manual reporting by approximately 3 hours per week" lands harder than "significant efficiency gains"
- Maintain a collaborative, partnership tone — "we" and "together" signal shared ownership of outcomes
- Keep paragraphs short — 3–4 sentences maximum per paragraph
- Lead with progress and wins before introducing risks or open items

### Never

- Minimize genuine risks or write around them — customers trust transparency
- Use blame language ("the customer hasn't adopted...", "your team has not...")
- Over-promise recovery timelines or outcomes you are not confident in
- Include internal CSM commentary about account health, scoring, or risk flags
- Reference plan mechanics the customer doesn't need to know ("your account is At Risk in our system")
- Use jargon: OCV, MoS, CSAT score, health score, milestone_updates, or any internal tool names

### Placeholder conventions

Throughout this document, placeholders appear in square brackets. Replace with specific content when generating a summary:

| Placeholder | Replace with |
|-------------|--------------|
| `[account_name]` | Customer's company name (display_account) |
| `[csm_name]` | CSM's name |
| `[plan_objective]` | Primary stated objective from the success plan canvas |
| `[benefit_1]`, `[benefit_2]` | Specific benefits from `key_benefits_realized` list |
| `[milestone_name]` | Specific milestone name as it appears in the plan |
| `[target_date]` | Recovery or completion target date |
| `[metric]` | Quantitative target or current measurement |
| `[blocker_summary]` | Brief, customer-appropriate description of the risk or blocker |

## 2. Template A — Positive Tone (All or Most Milestones On Track)

**Use when:** All milestones are `On Track`, or when milestones are On Track and at least one OCV outcome is `Delivered` or `In Progress`.

**Escalation threshold:** Threshold 1 — Standard monitoring.

---

### Template A1 — All On Track, Key Benefits Realized (recommended variant)

```
Hi [account_name] team,

We wanted to share a quick progress update on your success plan.

Things are moving well. [benefit_1]. [benefit_2]. These are the kinds of outcomes we set out to achieve together, and it's great to see them taking shape.

Your team is on track across all active milestones. [Optional: call out one milestone by name if particularly strong — e.g., "In particular, [milestone_name] is ahead of schedule, which positions you well for [next phase or outcome]."]

Next up: [brief summary of what the next milestone or phase involves and what the customer needs to do or prepare]. We're here to support you every step of the way.

Warm regards,
[csm_name]
```

---

### Template A2 — All On Track, No Key Benefits to Surface Yet

```
Hi [account_name] team,

We're excited to share an update on where things stand with your success plan.

Your team is making solid progress across all active milestones. [Briefly name 1–2 specific milestones and their current state — e.g., "The admin onboarding milestone is complete, and your team is actively building habits in [core module]."]

We're on a good path toward [plan_objective]. The next milestone we'll be working through together is [milestone_name], and here's what that involves: [one sentence on what the customer should expect or prepare].

Looking forward to continuing this momentum with you.

Warm regards,
[csm_name]
```

---

### Template A3 — OCV Outcome Delivered

```
A quick note on one specific win we want to make sure doesn't go unnoticed: [OCV outcome name or benefit description]. This is a direct result of the work your team put into [brief context — e.g., "deploying the reporting module and training your ops leads"].

[Optional: add a supporting detail or customer quote if available from notes.]
```

*This snippet can be inserted into Template A1 or A2 to highlight a delivered outcome.*

## 3. Template B — Cautionary Tone (Mixed Status: 1–2 At Risk)

**Use when:** 1–2 milestones are `At Risk`; no milestones are `Missed`. Progress is real but intervention is indicated.

**Escalation threshold:** Threshold 2 or 3 — Active management required.

---

### Template B1 — 1 At Risk Milestone, Solid Overall Progress

```
Hi [account_name] team,

We wanted to share a progress update and flag one area that needs our attention.

First, the good news: [milestone_name] and [other milestone(s) if applicable] are on track, and [benefit_1 if available]. Your team is making real progress toward [plan_objective].

That said, we want to be transparent about [at-risk milestone_name]. [Brief, non-blaming description of the current state — e.g., "Adoption in the operations team is running behind pace, currently at [metric] against a target of [metric]."] This is something we want to get ahead of together before it affects your timeline.

Here's what we'd like to do: [brief description of the proposed next step — e.g., "We'd like to schedule a focused conversation with your ops lead to understand what's getting in the way and identify the right support."] [csm_name] will be in touch this week to get that scheduled.

Thanks for your continued partnership — we're confident we can get this back on track.

Warm regards,
[csm_name]
```

---

### Template B2 — 2 At Risk Milestones, Recommending Alignment Call

```
Hi [account_name] team,

We wanted to take a moment to share an honest progress update.

There are areas of real momentum: [milestone_name] is on track, and [benefit_1 if available]. At the same time, we're tracking two milestones where we see elevated risk: [at-risk milestone 1] and [at-risk milestone 2].

For [at-risk milestone 1]: [one sentence on current state and the specific concern].
For [at-risk milestone 2]: [one sentence on current state and the specific concern].

Given the combination of these two items, we'd like to recommend a short alignment call with your team to review the situation and agree on a path forward. We want to make sure we're supporting you in the right way and that both of these get the attention they need.

[csm_name] will reach out to schedule something in the next few days. Thank you for your ongoing commitment to this plan — we're in this together.

Warm regards,
[csm_name]
```

## 4. Template C — Escalation Tone (Missed Milestone or Multiple At Risk)

**Use when:** Any milestone is `Missed`, or 3+ milestones are `At Risk`.

**Escalation threshold:** Threshold 4 or 5 — Recovery planning required / Executive escalation.

**Tone guidance:** Escalation tone does not mean alarm or blame. It means being clear, direct, and outcome-focused. Acknowledge the situation honestly, take shared ownership where appropriate, and focus the message on recovery and next steps. Avoid language that minimizes a genuine miss or that overpromises a recovery outcome.

---

### Template C1 — Single Missed Milestone, Recovery Plan in Place

```
Hi [account_name] team,

We wanted to reach out with a straightforward update on your success plan progress, including an honest conversation about one area where we've fallen behind.

[milestone_name] has not been completed by the planned date. [Brief explanation of what happened — e.g., "The integration timeline was extended due to [blocker_summary]."] We want to be transparent about this and share what we're doing to address it.

Here's our proposed path forward: [brief recovery plan — revised target, what the customer needs to do, what the CSM team will support]. We're aiming to have this back on track by [target_date].

On the positive side, [milestone_name(s) that are On Track] remain on track[, and [benefit_1 if available]]. We haven't lost momentum across the full plan.

We'd like to schedule time to walk through the recovery plan together and make sure we have your alignment. [csm_name] will follow up to arrange that.

Thank you for your partnership — we're committed to getting this to the right outcome for your team.

Warm regards,
[csm_name]
```

---

### Template C2 — Multiple Missed or At Risk, Requesting Executive Alignment

```
Hi [account_name] team,

We want to be straightforward with you about where your success plan stands and what we believe is the right next step.

We are tracking [n] milestone(s) as missed or at risk: [list milestone names briefly with one-line status each]. Together, these represent a meaningful deviation from the plan timeline.

This is not the trajectory we planned for, and we take our part in getting it back on course seriously. [Optional: one sentence of honest context on what contributed — shared ownership, no blame.]

Given the scope of the situation, we'd like to request a focused alignment conversation with [your sponsor / your leadership team] to review the full picture, agree on a revised path, and confirm continued commitment to [plan_objective]. We believe this is the right forum to make the decisions needed to move forward effectively.

[csm_name] will reach out directly to schedule this conversation. In the meantime, please don't hesitate to contact us with any questions.

We remain fully committed to your success.

Warm regards,
[csm_name]
```

---

### Template C — Key Benefits Realized Insert (escalation context)

When `key_benefits_realized` is provided in an escalation context, insert this paragraph after the opening context but before the recovery plan:

```
It's also important to acknowledge what your team has already achieved: [benefit_1]. [benefit_2 if available]. These are real outcomes — and they are part of why getting the full plan back on track matters.
```

## 5. QBR Pre-Work Note Structure

The QBR Pre-Work Note is an internal CSM document (not sent to the customer). It prepares the CSM and account team for a Quarterly Business Review conversation.

**Use when:** `include_qbr_note: true`

**Tone:** Professional, direct, internal — this is preparation material, not customer communication.

---

### QBR Pre-Work Note Template

```markdown
## QBR Pre-Work Note

**Account:** [account_name]  
**Review Date:** [review_date]  
**CSM:** [csm_name]  
**QBR Objective:** [One sentence on what the QBR is meant to accomplish — confirm plan progress, resolve risk, renew alignment, etc.]

---

### Milestone Summary

| Status | Count |
|--------|-------|
| On Track | [n] |
| At Risk | [n] |
| Missed | [n] |

[If any milestones are At Risk or Missed, include a one-line summary of the most significant item(s) here.]

---

### Key Wins to Highlight

[List 2–4 concrete wins to lead with in the QBR. Drawn from key_benefits_realized and On Track milestones. Frame in customer value terms.]

- [Win 1 — specific and customer-outcome-oriented]
- [Win 2]
- [Win 3 if available]

---

### Open Risks and Discussion Items

[List the items that require customer input, decision, or acknowledgment during the QBR. For each, note the risk, why it matters, and what decision or action is needed from the customer.]

- **[Risk/item name]:** [Current state and why it matters]. Needed from customer: [specific ask — decision, resource, sponsor escalation, etc.]
- [Additional items as applicable]

---

### Suggested Discussion Agenda

1. Celebrate progress and wins — [estimated time: 5 min]
2. Milestone status review — [estimated time: 10 min]
3. [Risk/open item 1] — discussion and decision — [estimated time: 10 min]
4. [Risk/open item 2 if applicable] — [estimated time: 5–10 min]
5. Revised plan / next 90 days — alignment and commitment — [estimated time: 10 min]

---

### Preparation Notes

[Any internal context the CSM or account team needs to be aware of before the QBR. This section is for internal eyes only — do not share with the customer.]

[Examples: "Sponsor changed last month — new contact not yet familiar with plan details. Budget for Q3 still unconfirmed. Engineering lead is internal champion — keep them close."]
```

## 6. Key Benefits Realized — Customer-Facing Language Guide

When surfacing `key_benefits_realized` in the Customer-Facing Summary, use the following guidance to translate internal CSM notes into customer-appropriate language.

### Framing principles

- Lead with the outcome the customer experienced, not the feature or activity that enabled it
- Use approximate quantification where the CSM has provided it — "approximately 3 hours per week" is more credible than "significant time savings"
- Attribute to the customer's team where possible — "your ops team" or "your sales leadership" — this reinforces their agency in the outcome
- Keep each benefit to 1–2 sentences in the customer summary; expand in the QBR note

### Transformation examples

| CSM internal note | Customer-facing language |
|-------------------|--------------------------|
| "Reporting dashboard deployed" | "Your operations team no longer needs to manually compile weekly reports — the data is now available in real time." |
| "VP Sales confirmed pipeline visibility as a win" | "Your VP Sales has already seen the impact of real-time pipeline visibility — a direct result of the work your team put in to configure the dashboard." |
| "3 admins certified, no support tickets in 30 days" | "Your three admin users are fully self-sufficient. In the past 30 days, your team has resolved onboarding questions independently — a sign the training has taken hold." |
| "Salesforce integration live" | "Your CRM and [platform] are now connected, which means your team is working from a single source of truth instead of managing two separate systems." |

### Length guidance

- Customer-Facing Summary: 1–3 benefit bullets or 1 short paragraph maximum
- QBR Pre-Work Note Key Wins section: up to 4 items, each 1–2 sentences
- Positive template variant (Template A1): weave 2 benefits into the opening paragraph naturally — do not list them mechanically
